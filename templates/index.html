    • Identifying Birds around the City of Houston

    • Do you love birds but have no idea how to identify them?

      Houstonians can identify birds around the city by using our bird identifier.

    • Drop images into our identifier to figure out what kind of bird you have spotted.

      Using our bird identifier you are able to find out what kind of bird you have found.


    • Great Blue Heron

      Great Blue Herons are the largest of the North American herons, they stand tall over wetlands and shores of open water. You can see these birds hanging out near the Buffalo Bayou.

    • Blue Jay

      Blue Jays are one of the most common backyard birds. They have white underparts, blue upperparts, a blue crest, a black necklace, eye line, and bill, a blue tail with dark bars and a white bar and flecking on its wings. Both male and females look alike.

    • Northern Flicker

      The Northern Flicker is a large woodpecker with an overall brownish appearance. Find this bird most likely in The Woodlands.
